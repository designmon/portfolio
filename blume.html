WHERE DID THE IDEA COME FROM?

The idea for the project came from looking at urban data. The rise in urban population is predited to increase in the next years. At the same time, cities are the areas that strongly struggle with air pollution.

The number of green public spaces in cities is decreasing. New developments rely on concrete for construction & design of public spaces rather than on fostering biodiversity & creating green public spaces. Thus, people try to create communities and cultivate indoor plants & urban gardens on their own to take care of their well-being and mental health while living in large cities.

Creating a digital tool for plant propagation community most likely cannot fix poor city administration and regulatory grey zones, but has potential to support community creation & empower people to live green within urban areas.

SOCIAL HOBBY

Plant care & propagation are social hobbies. The respondents were asked to choose three ways they learned plant propagation and most of them used online ressources, but as many as 60 out of 167 learned through friends and family. 80 respondents learned through social media.

Asked how they connected when swapping plants in the past, 38,9% respondents knew the person they swapped with but as many as 41,3% used social media groups to connect with others to exchange plants.

The collected data & the number of social media groups focused on plants, prove that plant care & propagation are social hobbies that strongly rely on community exchange of knowledge, experience, advice, items.

COMPETITION & MARKETING POTENTIAL

The most common apps used by the respondents were Planta (individual care schedule and reminders, step by step guides) and PictureThis (plant and disease recognition with plant care tips). There are also several other solutions available on the market, i.e. plant search apps and sensory hardware with care reminding software. The researched solutions focus mainly on learning and care management.

Indoor plant cultivation, especially plant propagation & indoor jungles are rising trends on social media platforms. Typically the user learnes through tutorials on YouTube, seeks advice & exchange opportunities on Facebook and Reddit groups and gets design inspiration from Instagram and Pinterest. Blogs and online articles are also an important knowledge source for plant propagators.

OPPORTUNITIES

Asked to select three features that a digital tool for plant propagation should have, the respondents chose the following:
1. Personal plant collection album (journal about your plants, add pictures etc.) - 71 out of 167 responses
2. Propagation wikipedia/library - 64 out of 167 responses
3. Plant swapping/exchange platform - 64 out of 167 responses

At the first glance the three top features present three different independent solutions. However, both the album/journal and swapping platform express a need for space to accomodate a community and exchange of knowledge.

How can plant care & propagation connect people online in a way that keeps them engaged long term?

The goal is to make Blume a go-to propagation & plant exchange app to help people grow their own indoor jungles & support an offline hobby.
With that goal in mind following questions were targeted in the sprint:
- How can plant exchange be made easy and pleasant?
- How to keep users motivated to continue engaging in an online exchange community?
- How can a digital tool complement an offline hobby?

04

PROTOTYPE

The goal of the first prototype was to test the results of the survey, the top two responses - personal plant journal and plant exchange.

COLLECTION

My collection is a personal plant album that allows to keep track of your plants & propagations - the ones you have at home (private) and those you want to swap, give away or sell (public).

Keep track of your house plants sorted by room in Collect tab. You can journal about each of your plants and add photos. Published tab shows you which plants you published to exchange.

EXCHANGE

Front screen of the exchange displays most recent exchange posts. User can quickly customize content according to their preferences through search bar and view results as a list or a map. Each list item contains content that is most relevant to the user, a photo as well as bookmarking and swap buttons.

SWAP PROCESS

The app organises swapping plants in steps. Chat option allows to discuss transaction details and pick-up. Swap option allows to directly choose a plant from your collection to swap for. Both users' collections get updated after the swap is finished.

ADD CONTENT

In-app post editor allows for consistent and organised content creation for both private and sharing purposes.

ROUND 1 RESULTS

+ POSITIVE
The users were generally happy about the plant exchange. Custom search bar, map & list view of offers, content bookmarking, following other users and in-app post editing received positive feedback. All testers expressed their preference for using a mobile app rather than Facebook Groups for swapping plants & propagations.

- NEGATIVE
The purpose of "My Plants" section was unclear to all users. Moreover, the testers expressed their lack of interest in journaling and documenting experience with their plants for private purposes. One of the users stated that they would be interested in writing posts to share with other community members, however described the experience as very similar to already existing Facebook Groups.

04

PROTOTYPE

Second prototype responds to users' feedback and creates an online exchange space capable of accomodating swapping process. The space was developed using swipe typology and creating a "Tinder for plants".

SWAP

Swipe right to like, swipe left to pass. Discover plants that others would like to swap within your local area.

Plant swapping is a fast transaction based on negotiations. The app allows plant owners to skip negotiations by helping users find matching plants in their collections.

INBOX

Inbox contains your matches and messages.

Matches are organized using list component. Each list item contains content that is most relevant to the user to quickly assess a potential transaction - swapper's info (name, rating, distance), subjects of transaction (plant name & type) and a timer displaying remaining time.

Every match disables plant visibility in "Swap" section for 24h. Within this time the user gets to respond by either canceling or swapping.

Swapping is organized in the following steps:
‍
1 - Send suggested pick-up spot, date and time.
‍
2 - Decide on the pick-up by:
2A - accepting received suggestion,
2B - suggesting an alternative pick-up,
2C - directly discussing alternatives through chat.
‍
3 - Once a pick-up is agreed upon and accepted, the timer pauses. The plant is marked as swapped.
‍
4 - After the arranged pick-up rate the fellow user to close the transaction and receive your rating.

PROFILE

Add content, manage settings and view reviews through the Profile tab.

In-app step-by-step content editor allows to quickly organize and add new plants & propagations that you'd like to swap.

Settings allow to customize swap discovery preferences - distance, relevant content, wish list, as well as app settings - notifications, disable plant visibility, distance units.
‍
Reviews button allows you to view your rating and all the reviews you've received and have given.

05

TEST

WHAT THE USERS SAID:

“Tinder for plants - I like it! I’d totally use it.”

“The app is so nice, I love the interface”

"I 'd like to have a full intro tutorial first. I've never used Tinder so I don't know how it works."

‍

ROUND 2 RESULTS

+ POSITIVE
The users really liked the swipe concept for swapping plants. Customizable profile and discovery settings, rating system and in-app post editor with tags were described as great functions. All users liked the simple UI which puts the main component - plants & propagations in the foreground.

- NEGATIVE
Even tough swapping steps were clear and understandable, all of the users expressed a need for a step-by-step tutorial during the first swap. A general app introduction was also described as not informative enough. The Inbox tab needed to be expanded to display and organize different kinds of matches as per users' feedback.

All in all, the concept received a very positive response. The goal for the next round is to focus on refining the concept and existing features.

06

LESSONS LEARNED

GENERAL

- Design Sprint
- User survey
- User Flows
- Prototyping
- Conducting 1:1 user tests (interview script, note taking, results mapping)

RESEARCH

-Do not add "Other:" as an option for every question
-State the number of responses in the question
- Avoid starting questions with if unless the survey supports skip logic. Reword to "How..." and add response "none of these".
- When asking about products, research market & add found solutions as responses rather than allowing the user to type anything.
- By every questions think if there may be a good follow up question for it.
- Add "N/A" or "none of these" as an option.

PROTOTYPING

- Build as interactive as possible for better tester understanding.
- Keep navigation consistent throughout the entire app - less (clicks) is more (user satisfaction).
